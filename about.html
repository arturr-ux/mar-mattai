About The Monastery

Mar Mattai monastery is located on the slope of an upside-down mountain called mount alfaf in Syriac (35-38km) away northeast of Mosul -Iraq It was founded in the fourth century around 363 by the monk Mar Mattai. The monastery took the name of Saint Matthew, as he was its founder, and he was appointed the first abbot of the monastery. Followed by many of monks and the monotheists of Nineveh, Iraq and Persia

A hundred years after the monastery was founded, it was exposed to a major fire that led to the obliteration of its features and the disappearance of the inscriptions and decorations. However, the Monastic life returned to it in the fifth century as the name of the monastery emerged in 544 and continued until 1171 but it was abandoned due to attacks by the residents of the region and remained abandoned for about 16 years. The monastery lived a lot of turbulent periods and it witnessed disturbances until the criminals inhabited it and expelled the monks from it and it was left

This cycle of disturbances and the return of life to the monastery continued until 1970 when father Isaac Saka restored the monastery to keep pace with development. After that, everyone who headed the monastery made minor modifications to it until we reached our current time, as there are no fixed historical documents that show us the shape and design of the first monastery or the plan for construction due to the changes and problems that the monastery went through but;

The parts that preserved their original shapes are:
Slaughterhouse and the Saints house
The cell of Saint Matthew, which has a special altar carved out of the rock.
The road leading to the monastery, called Al-Tabki
Some caves and steeples
Cisterns

Mar Mattai Monastery Digitalization

Mar Mattai Monastery is one of the most important monasteries in Iraq founded in 363. However, conflicts and problems occurred during the past years leading to the erasure of most of its features. The inscriptions and decorations are barely present now. Hence, our project aims to 3D scan the monastery and build a 3D model to preserve it for the next generations.
